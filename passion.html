The Psychological Aspects

üß† Identity and Self-Esteem Fans often identify with their favorite teams, intertwining their self-concept with the team's successes and failures. This phenomenon, known as Basking in Reflected Glory (BIRGing), leads fans to feel a sense of pride when their team wins, as if they themselves have achieved victory. Conversely, losses can lead to Cutting Off Reflected Failure (CORFing), where fans distance themselves from the team to protect their self-esteem.

ü§ù Sense of Belonging and Community Cricket fandom fosters a strong sense of community. Engaging in shared rituals, such as wearing team colors or chanting slogans, creates a collective identity among fans. This communal experience provides emotional support and a sense of belonging, which are crucial for mental well-being.
